The Liberation of the Engineer

AI doesn't replace engineers—it elevates them.

Old Reality New Reality
Boilerplate code AI generates scaffolds while you architect
Manual testing of edge cases AI-assisted test generation with strategic oversight
Documentation written after the fact AI generates documentation you refine
Debugging syntax errors Focus on system design and user experience
Grunt work consuming time Strategic work creating value

The Shift: From "implementer" to "strategist and director." Your creativity becomes the scarce resource—and that's what companies will pay for.

Think about what engineering has traditionally been: endless configuration, repetitive debugging of syntax errors, boilerplate code that numbs the mind, documentation that takes more time than the solution itself. AI changes this equation fundamentally. With AI, you're not spending 80% of your time on grunt work. You're spending 80% of your time on what matters: architecture, design, user experience, solving novel problems. This is a massive shift in what it means to be an engineer. You're elevated from "implementer" to "strategist and director." The thing that will be scarce and valuable is your creativity, your judgment, your vision for how systems should work—not your ability to type code. This is the liberation. The boring stuff becomes automated. Your unique human capability becomes the bottleneck, and that's precisely where you should be spending your time.

Professional Preparation

  1. Learn AI deeply
    Not superficially. Understand the fundamentals of how these systems work, their limitations, their potential.
  2. Maintain ethical standards
    AI amplifies intent. If you use it for good, it multiplies the good. Stay grounded in gospel principles.
  3. Develop creativity
    The boring code will be automated. Your value is in insight, vision, and solving problems that matter.
  4. Stay grounded in doctrine
    Technology is a tool. The gospel is the foundation. Keep that relationship clear.
Practically, what does this mean for you? How do you prepare for this moment professionally? First, learn AI deeply, not superficially. Don't just use ChatGPT. Understand how these models work, what they can do, what their limitations are, what the research frontier looks like. Be a student of the field. Second, maintain ethical standards. This is crucial. AI amplifies whatever intent you feed into it. If you use it to multiply good, it will. If you use it for harm, it will do that too. Stay grounded in the principles that matter—honesty, integrity, service to others. Third, develop creativity. This is what I said earlier: the boring code is being automated. Your value as an engineer is going to be your ability to imagine solutions to problems that don't have obvious answers. That's where creativity matters. And fourth, stay grounded in doctrine. Don't let the technology seduce you into thinking it's more important than it is. It's a tool. It's powerful, but it's a tool. The gospel—your relationship with Heavenly Father, your discipleship to Jesus Christ—that's the foundation. Keep that as your north star.
