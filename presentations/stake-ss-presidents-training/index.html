Duties of the Ward Sunday School Presidency

General Handbook, Section 13

Key Duties — General Handbook §13

  • Serve on the ward council
  • Oversee home- and church-centered gospel learning
  • Support and instruct teachers — observe, give feedback
  • Organize and oversee Sunday School classes
  • Encourage outreach to non-attenders
  • Help members make and keep sacred covenants
  • Recommend teachers to the bishopric
  • Help implement Come, Follow Me
Discussion Question
How can we more effectively fulfill these duties in our individual wards?

Teaching in the Savior's Way

Core principles of Christlike teaching

Core Principles

  • Love — The Savior saw each person as an individual of infinite worth
  • Simplicity — Teach clear doctrine; avoid complexity that distracts
  • Questions — Ask inspired questions that invite reflection and revelation
  • Invitations — Invite learners to act, not just to know
  • Spirit-led — Yield to promptings; the Spirit is the real teacher
Share a Moment
Can 1–2 presidents share a brief example of Christlike teaching they witnessed — in Sunday School or elsewhere?

Connecting Teaching to the Handbook

"Christlike teachers love the people they teach, help them feel the Spirit, and inspire them to become more like the Savior."
— Teaching in the Savior's Way
  • We are not just dispensing information — we are inviting conversion
  • The handbook asks us to help teachers improve learning and teaching
  • Our role: observe, encourage, and develop the teachers in our wards

Focus on the One

Not Just the Ninety and Nine

The Parable of the Lost Sheep

Luke 15:3–7

"What man of you, having an hundred sheep, if he lose one of them, doth not leave the ninety and nine in the wilderness, and go after that which is lost, until he find it?"
— Luke 15:4
  • Every class member matters — especially those who are absent
  • Sunday School presidencies can model and inspire personal ministering
  • Reach out to the lost, the wandering, and the newly moved-in

Practical Ideas: Reaching the One

In Sunday School Classes

  • Assign a classmate to follow up with absentees
  • Save a seat; send a text: "We missed you"
  • Ask teachers to note who is struggling or disengaged

As a Presidency

  • Review attendance trends with the ward council
  • Coordinate with ministering brothers and sisters
  • Make personal calls to inactive youth or adults
Group Sharing
What practical ideas have worked in your ward for reaching the one?

Miracles of Our Day

"Do Not Miss the Majesty of This Moment"

President Nelson's Witness

"Do you see what is happening right before our eyes? I pray that we will not miss the majesty of this moment! The Lord is indeed hastening His work."
— President Russell M. Nelson, October 2024 General Conference
  • We live in a time of unprecedented spiritual and temporal blessings
  • The gathering of Israel is accelerating — we are part of it
  • New tools are being placed in our hands for a reason
  • Our responsibility: use these tools with discernment and the Spirit

AI & Technology as Modern Aids

  • Gospel study: Use AI to explore scriptures, cross-references, historical context
  • Lesson preparation: Generate discussion questions, find quotes, summarize talks
  • Personalized outreach: Craft thoughtful messages to class members
  • Come, Follow Me: Deeper preparation in less time
Key Principle
Technology serves the Spirit — it never replaces it. Let promptings lead; let tools assist.
"We have been given these miraculous resources to help us in our sacred responsibilities."
— General Handbook

Elevating Teaching

Home-Centered, Church-Supported Gospel Learning

Raising Teaching Quality

  • Teach doctrine and principles — not just stories or activities
  • Connect Sunday learning to daily living and Come, Follow Me
  • Invite learners to feel the Spirit, not just hear information
  • Application: "What will you do this week because of what we discussed?"
Home-Centered Model
The Church supports what happens at home — not the other way around. Sunday School reinforces and inspires family gospel learning.

Supporting Teachers & Families

Supporting Teachers

  • Observe classes periodically
  • Offer specific, kind feedback
  • Share Teaching in the Savior's Way resources
  • Ensure teachers feel valued and trained

Supporting Families

  • Encourage Come, Follow Me at home
  • Share ideas in ward council
  • Equip parents to be the primary gospel teachers
  • Celebrate family learning wins
