Your Sacred Calling

Roles & Responsibilities — General Handbook, §13

"The Sunday School helps accomplish God's work of salvation and exaltation."
Transition statement: "Everything we do in Sunday School flows from one purpose — to help God's children come unto Christ." THOUGHT-PROVOKING: Before we look at the handbook, what do you believe your single most important duty is right now?

The Purpose of Sunday School (§13)

  • Help accomplish God's work of salvation and exaltation
  • Strengthen faith in Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ by teaching "the doctrine of the kingdom" (D&C 88:77)
  • Support home-centered, Church-supported gospel learning
  • Help members teach in the Savior's way
"The aim of all gospel learning and teaching is to deepen our conversion and help us become more like Jesus Christ."
— Come, Follow Me Introductory Materials
This is the WHY before the WHAT. Every handbook duty flows from this Christ-centered purpose. THOUGHT-PROVOKING: On a scale of 1–10, how Christ-centered does your Sunday School currently feel? What would move it one point higher?

a. Elevating Teaching Quality in the Ward

  • Observe classes periodically — sit in, take notes, be present
  • Offer specific, kind, private feedback after class
  • Ensure every teacher knows and uses Teaching in the Savior's Way
  • Focus on doctrine and principles — not just stories or activities
  • Ask teachers: "What will your students feel moved to do this week?"
"Invite learners to feel the Spirit, not just hear information."
— Teaching in the Savior's Way
Closing Question for Every Lesson
"What will you do this week because of what we discussed?"
Discussion
What is one specific thing you could do in the next 30 days to elevate teaching in your ward?
THOUGHT-PROVOKING: Think of the best teacher you've ever had — in Church or in life. What made them memorable? Were they teaching information, or were they teaching YOU? Key insight: our job is not to produce entertaining classes. It's to create environments where the Spirit can teach. Practical commitment: encourage each president to schedule one class observation in the next 30 days. Just one. Then give feedback.

b. Teacher Council Meetings — Coordinating with Ward Council

  • Held quarterly during the 50-minute class time — ward council schedules it
  • Usually led by the Sunday School president; primary resource: Teaching in the Savior's Way
  • Also organize Teacher Council Meetings for Parents — to equip home gospel teachers
The Teacher Council Meeting is one of our most powerful tools — and most underutilized. THOUGHT-PROVOKING: How many of you have held a Teacher Council Meeting in the last 6 months? What was the feeling in the room? For those who haven't: what is the primary barrier? Let's problem-solve that right now. Elder Bednar: "Revelation is scattered among the various members of a council. As an issue comes forward, we need, invite, and hear counsel from everyone." — Liahona, June 2022

c. Introducing New Teachers to Teaching in the Savior's Way

  • Every new teacher deserves a personal orientation from the presidency — within 2 weeks
  • Walk through the Teaching in the Savior's Way booklet together
  • Explain the four principles: Focus on Christ · Teach the Doctrine · Invite Diligent Learning · Teach by the Spirit
  • Invite them to observe an experienced teacher before their first lesson
  • Follow up after lesson 1 — celebrate and encourage
Suggested First Meeting
"What are you most excited about? What feels most challenging?" Then listen.
THOUGHT-PROVOKING: Think of a new teacher in your ward right now. When did you last meet with them one-on-one? Do they know Teaching in the Savior's Way exists? The most critical window for a new teacher is the first 90 days. Good habits formed early stick. Bad habits calcify. Practical tip: Create a simple "new teacher welcome packet" — the booklet, a brief welcome note, a one-pager of key principles. Takes 30 minutes to make, lasts a lifetime.

d. Helping Parents Understand Teaching in the Savior's Way

  • The home is the primary classroom — Sunday School supports it, not the reverse
  • Parents are the most important gospel teachers their children will ever have
  • Organize a Teacher Council Meeting for Parents — same quarterly format
  • Help parents apply Teaching in the Savior's Way principles at the dinner table, in family prayer, during Come, Follow Me
  • Share family learning success stories in ward council
Discussion
If you could send one message to every parent in your ward about gospel teaching at home — what would it be?
The Teacher Council Meeting for Parents is a hidden gem in the handbook. Most wards have never done one. THOUGHT-PROVOKING: Imagine what could happen if parents felt equipped and inspired to teach at home. How would that change your Sunday School class dynamics? What would be different? Practical: Bring this proposal to your bishop. Ask him to introduce it from the pulpit so it has weight and visibility.

Teaching in the Savior's Way

All things center on Jesus Christ

"I am the way, the truth, and the life." — John 14:6
Before advancing — pause. Ask the room: "When you think of how the Savior taught, what comes to mind first?" Let 2–3 people respond. Their answers will shape how you present the next slides.

The Four Principles of Christlike Teaching

  • Love Those You Teach — see each learner the way Christ sees them
  • Focus on Jesus Christ — He is the center of every lesson
  • Teach the Doctrine — clearly, simply, powerfully
  • Invite Diligent Learning — create space for the Spirit to teach
  • Teach by the Spirit — yield to promptings; He is the real teacher
Every principle converges at one point →
Deepened conversion to Jesus Christ
Walk through the venn diagram together. The key insight is the CENTER — it is not any one principle alone, but all of them pointing toward deepened conversion to Jesus Christ. THOUGHT-PROVOKING: Which of these four is your ward's greatest strength right now? Which is the greatest growth opportunity? Follow-up: "How would you help a teacher who loves their class but is teaching stories and activities instead of doctrine?"

He Is the Model

"Come, follow me."
— Luke 18:22
  • He knew those He taught — their names, struggles, potential
  • He asked questions that invited reflection and revelation
  • He used simple stories — truths anyone could remember
  • He always invited action — "go and do," "arise," "follow me"
This image is from the Book of Mormon film — the resurrected Christ among the Nephites. He came TO them. He didn't wait for them to come to Him. THOUGHT-PROVOKING: Who in your ward needs the Savior to "come to them" right now through the teaching of Sunday School? What would that actually look like? "Our job is not to copy the Savior's curriculum. It's to embody His approach: love, simplicity, inspired questions, invitations, and Spirit."

Ministering to the One

"Go after that which is lost, until he find it."

Luke 15:4
Before advancing — pause and ask: "Who is your 'one' right now? Is there a face coming to mind — someone who used to come, who drifted, who you haven't seen in months?" Give people a moment to sit with that. Then advance.

The Parable of the Lost Sheep

Luke 15:3–7

"What man of you, having an hundred sheep, if he lose one of them, doth not leave the ninety and nine in the wilderness, and go after that which is lost, until he find it? And when he hath found it, he layeth it on his shoulders, rejoicing."
— Luke 15:4–5
  • Every class member matters — especially those who are absent
  • Sunday School presidencies can model and inspire personal ministering
  • Reach the wandering, the struggling, the newly moved in
The Savior left the ninety and nine. He didn't send a letter. He didn't delegate. He went personally. THOUGHT-PROVOKING: What percentage of your ward's class roster actually shows up on a given Sunday? Who are the missing ones? Do they even know they are missed? "A simple text that says 'We missed you today' can change a life — especially when it comes from the right person at the right moment."

Practical: Reaching the One

In Sunday School Classes

  • Assign a classmate to follow up with every absentee
  • Teachers send a personal: "We missed you today"
  • Save a seat — make absence visible and return celebrated
  • Ask teachers to notice who is disengaged or struggling

As a Presidency

  • Review attendance trends in ward council monthly
  • Coordinate with ministering brothers and sisters
  • Make personal calls to inactive youth and adults
  • Celebrate every return — loudly and privately
Share Together
What practical idea has worked in your ward for reaching the one? What happened when you reached out?
THOUGHT-PROVOKING: What is the difference between someone who leaves and never returns — and someone who leaves but comes back? Often the answer is: one person reached out. One person said "you matter to us." Challenge: Can each presidency tonight commit to identifying ONE person to reach out to this week?

Miracles of Our Day

President Nelson's Witness to Our Generation

Transition: "I want to share two things President Nelson has said that I cannot get out of my mind. They have changed how I see this work." Read them slowly. With weight. These are not bullet points. These are prophetic declarations.

President Nelson: His Mightiest Works

"I know things about what lies ahead that I cannot share. But I can tell you that the Lord will do some of His mightiest works between now and when He comes again."
— President Russell M. Nelson
  • We are not spectators — we are participants in these mightiest works
  • Every converted soul, every faithful teacher, every person reached — is part of that work
  • The Sunday School presidency is a front-line instrument in the Lord's hands
Read this quote slowly. Then be quiet for 10 seconds. Let it settle. THOUGHT-PROVOKING: What do you think "His mightiest works" might look like in your ward? In a Sunday School class? In the life of one student? This is a call to vision and courage. Our work is not small. It is not routine. It is part of something the Lord has been preparing since before the world was organized.

President Nelson: The Majesty of This Moment

"Do you see what is happening right before our eyes? I pray that we will not miss the majesty of this moment! The Lord is indeed hastening His work."
— President Russell M. Nelson, General Conference, October 2024
  • We live in the most extraordinary era in the history of the world
  • The gathering of Israel is accelerating — we are part of it
  • New tools, new revelation, new resources — placed in our hands for a reason
  • The question is not are miracles happening? — it is are we noticing them?
Reflection
Where have you personally seen the Lord hasten His work recently — in your ward, your family, your calling?
President Nelson asks: "Do you SEE what is happening?" Not: do you know about it intellectually — but do you actually SEE it? Feel it? THOUGHT-PROVOKING: What would change about how you fulfill this calling if you truly believed you were part of the Lord's mightiest, most majestic work in all of history? You are not managing a church program. You are helping gather Israel. That is the work of angels — and you said yes to it.
