Anointed with Oil

Day 4 of Easter Week. Wednesday is a quiet day in the scriptural record — a day of retreat at Bethany before the final storm. A woman, identified by John as Mary, anoints Jesus with costly spikenard, preparing His body for burial even before the cross. The disciples were indignant; Jesus was moved. Mary of Bethany pours costly spikenard over Jesus — an alabaster box broken open, the fragrance filling the whole house. The disciples saw waste; Jesus saw worship. He saw in her act a preparation for what was to come.
"There came unto him a woman having an alabaster box of very precious ointment, and poured it on his head, as he sat at meat…

"Then took Mary a pound of ointment of spikenard, very costly, and anointed the feet of Jesus, and wiped his feet with her hair: and the house was filled with the odour of the ointment…

"She hath done what she could: she is come aforehand to anoint my body to the burying."
— Matthew 26:7 · John 12:3 · Mark 14:8
"She hath done what she could." Jesus defended her when no one else would. He called her act a preparation for burial — she was perhaps the only one who truly understood what was coming. And He promised: "Wheresoever this gospel shall be preached throughout the whole world, this also that she hath done shall be spoken of for a memorial of her." (Mark 14:9)
