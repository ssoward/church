Prayer Self-Assessment

Easy 15-20 minutes

Compare your personal prayers to both the Zoramite prayer and Alma's prayer to identify areas for improvement.

Materials Needed:
  • Scripture journal or notebook
  • Quiet space for reflection
  • Pen or pencil
Steps:
  1. Read Alma 31:15-18 (Zoramite prayer) and 31:26-35 (Alma's prayer)
  2. Write down characteristics of each prayer
  3. Reflect on your recent prayers - which do they resemble more?
  4. Identify 2-3 specific improvements you can make
  5. Practice a new style of prayer incorporating these insights

Reflection Questions:

  • What motivates my prayers - genuine need or habit?
  • How often do I pray for others versus myself?
  • Do I truly believe God hears and answers my prayers?

Pride Inventory

Medium 30-45 minutes

Honest self-examination to identify areas where pride may be affecting your spiritual growth and relationships.

Steps:
  1. List the ways Zoramites showed pride (verses 24-28)
  2. For each area, honestly assess your own tendencies
  3. Identify your personal "Rameumptoms" - areas where you seek recognition
  4. Write specific goals for developing humility
  5. Plan concrete actions to implement these changes

Pride Indicators to Consider:

  • Comparing yourself to others
  • Difficulty accepting correction
  • Seeking recognition for good deeds
  • Judging others' spiritual worthiness
  • Focusing on external religious performance

Word Power Study

Medium 25-35 minutes

Deep dive into Alma 31:5 and the power of God's word to transform hearts and minds.

Cross-Reference Study:
  1. Study Alma 31:5 in depth
  2. Look up cross-references about God's word (Heb. 4:12, Isa. 55:11, Alma 32:28)
  3. Journal about times God's word has changed your heart
  4. Identify someone who needs the power of God's word
  5. Plan how you can share appropriate scriptures with them

Compassion Challenge

Medium Ongoing

Follow Alma's example by developing Christ-like compassion for those who have lost their way spiritually.

Weekly Challenge:
  1. Identify someone struggling with faith or making poor choices
  2. Study Alma's response to the Zoramites (verses 24-35)
  3. Pray for that person daily using Alma's prayer as a model
  4. Look for opportunities to show love without judgment
  5. Journal about insights gained through this practice

Build Your Own Rameumptom

Easy 20-30 minutes

Physical object lesson using blocks or household items to build a "Rameumptom" and discuss its symbolism.

Materials:
  • Building blocks, books, or boxes
  • Small figure or toy person
  • Timer (optional)
Activity:
  1. Read Alma 31:12-14 together
  2. Build a tall structure that fits only one person on top
  3. Have family members take turns standing on it
  4. Discuss how it feels to be elevated above others
  5. Compare to humble prayer on the ground
  6. Talk about ways we might build "Rameumptoms" in our lives

Prayer Drama

Medium 15-25 minutes

Act out the contrast between Zoramite and Alma's prayers to understand the difference between true and false worship.

Activity:
  1. Assign roles: Zoramites, Alma, narrator
  2. Act out the Zoramite prayer (verses 15-18) with exaggerated pride
  3. Contrast with Alma's heartfelt prayer (verses 26-35)
  4. Discuss the differences in tone, content, and motivation
  5. Practice family prayers using Alma's example

Sunday School Lesson

Medium 40-50 minutes

Complete lesson plan for teaching Alma 31 to youth or adult classes.

Lesson Outline:
  1. Opening (5 min): Show picture of a tower or tall building. Discuss why people build high structures.
  2. Context (10 min): Brief overview of Zoramite apostasy and Alma's concern.
  3. Rameumptom Activity (15 min): Have class analyze the Zoramite prayer and discuss problems.
  4. Contrast (15 min): Compare with Alma's prayer, highlighting differences.
  5. Application (15 min): Discuss modern applications and personal commitments.
Teaching Materials:
  • Pictures of towers/tall buildings
  • Handout comparing prayers
  • Whiteboard/markers
  • Small step stool or platform (optional)
